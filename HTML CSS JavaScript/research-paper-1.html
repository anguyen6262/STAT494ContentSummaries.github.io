Genome-wide association study of 14,000 cases of seven common diseases and 3,000 shared controls

  • Single Nucleotide Polymorphisms(SNP): genomic variant at a single base position in DNA.
  • Aetiologies: cause of disease.
  • Genetic basis: the extent of variance in an individual at risk for contracting a common disease based on heredity.
  • Allelic architecture:# of distinct alleles that affect the likelihood of contracting a disease at a disease locus(location of a specific gene) and their frequencies.

The following study was written in 2007 and is still referenced today in the field of genetic Statistics. They sampled Caucuasian people in Europe. The reasoning for this sample is because it is easier to test people that are genetically similar. This can be controversial because the data and results can't be accuratly applied to people with different ethnic backgrounds. The dataset is used for future studies because it is more cost effective to reuse datasets rather than collect new samples. They focused on seven different diseases and tested whether or not they were associated with a SNPs. Descriptions of the seven diseases can be found below. this

  • Bipolar disorder : Episodes of extreme changes in mood, energy level, motivation, and interests.
  • Coronary artery disease : damage or disease in the heart's blood vessels which can lead to heart attacks or chest pain.
  • Crohn’s disease : Bowel disease that causes abdominal pain, diarrhea, and weight loss.
  • Hypertension : High blood pressure. Can lead to heart disease or strokes.
  • Rheumatoid arthritis : Body’s immune system attacks its own tissues. Symptoms include swelling or inflammation of joints.
  • Type 1 Diabetes: diagnosed in childhood. Symptoms include thirst, hunger, and fatigue. The body doesn’t produce enough insulin.
  • Type 2 Diabetes: diagnosed in adult years. Symptoms include thirst, hunger, and fatigue. The body doesn’t produce enough insulin.
