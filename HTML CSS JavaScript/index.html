    Content Summary 1

    • Single Nucleotide Polymorphisms(SNP): genomic variant at a single base position in DNA.
    • Phenotypes: Features that can be observed externally. (ex: tall height)
    • Genotypes: Genetic make up that can’t be observed. Can’t tell if being is homozygous or heterozygous for a character. Also can be thought of as set of genes for a particular trait.
    • Homozygous: Same set of genes. Both dominant or both recessive.
    • Heterozygous: Different set of genes. Has both dominant and recessive alleles.

    • Bipolar disorder (BD) : Episodes of extreme changes in mood, energy level, motivation, and interests.
    • Coronary artery disease (CAD): damage or disease in the heart's blood vessels which can lead to heart attacks or chest pain.
    • Crohn’s disease (CD): Bowel disease that causes abdominal pain, diarrhea, and weight loss.
    • Hypertension (HT): High blood pressure. Can lead to heart disease or strokes.
    • Rheumatoid arthritis (RA): Body’s immune system attacks its own tissues. Symptoms include swelling or inflammation of joints.
    • Type 1 Diabetes: diagnosed in childhood. Symptoms include thirst, hunger, and fatigue. The body doesn’t produce enough insulin.
    • Type 2 Diabetes: diagnosed in adult years. Symptoms include thirst, hunger, and fatigue. The body doesn’t produce enough insulin.
