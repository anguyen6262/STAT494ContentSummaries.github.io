Genetics Background Knowledge

How Are Organisms Created?

A body is composed of trillions of cells. Inside of a cell is a nucleus which contains chromatids. Chromatids are tangled and chromosomes are coiled chromatids in the process of cell division. Chromatids and chromosomes are similar, but are different names for different structures. Chromosomes are composed of genes which are made out of deoxyriboNucleotide Acid (DNA). DNA is essentially a blueprint to creating organisms. From eye color to the number of appendages. DNA consists of a sequence of four different blocks called nucleotides. The four types of nucleotides are Adenine(A), Cytosine(C), Thymine(T), and Guanine(G). These are usually denoted as their respective first letters. A only pairs with T and C only pairs with G. In a sequence of nucleotides if there is variation we call that variation a single nucleotide polymorphism (snps).

Heredity

Heredity is passing down traits from parents to their children. A trait is a genetic characteristic about an individual such as eye color, height, and hair type. Traits are determined by genes. Even though siblings get half of their DNA from their father and the other half from their mother, each child has a different combination. This is because the parent’s genes are shuffled before they are passed down to their children. This is called recombination.

Alleles are a form of genes and are used to predict traits. A set of alleles is homozygous if both alleles are both dominant or both recessive. If a set of alleles contains one dominant and one recessive allele then the set is heterozygous. Recessive traits are only expressed when both alleles are recessive. If even one allele is dominant then the dominant trait will be expressed.

Genotype

Genotypes are the genetic make up that can’t be observed. Whereas phenotypes are features that can be observed externally such as eye color. Genotyping is the process of recording an individual's sequence of nucleotides at different locations.
