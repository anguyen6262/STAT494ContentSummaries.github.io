Genome-wide association study of 14,000 cases of seven common diseases and 3,000 shared controls

Key Definitions

  • Genetic basis: the extent of variance in an individual at risk for contracting a common disease based on heredity. Morbidity: suffering from a medical condition. Genotyping: process of determining DNA sequence. This process helps scientists see genetic variations. Aetiologies: cause of disease. Allelic architecture:# of distinct alleles that affect the likelihood of contracting a disease at a disease locus(location of a specific gene) and their frequencies. Phenotyping: process of determining, analyzing, and predicting phenotypes. Allele frequency: Calculated by dividing the # of times the allele of interest is observed by the total number of alleles at that locus. Linkage: Closeness of genes or other DNA sequences to one another on the same chromosome. Linkage Disequilibrium: non-random association of alleles at different loci in a given population. Covariates: independent variables that can affect outcome of trial. Single Nucleotide Polymorphisms(SNP): genomic variant at a single base position in DNA. Pathological: caused or altered by disease.

The following study was written in 2007 and is still referenced today in the field of genetic Statistics. They sampled Caucuasian people in Europe. The reasoning for this sample is because it is easier to test people that are genetically similar. This can be controversial because the data and results can't be accuratly applied to people with different ethnic backgrounds. The dataset is used for future studies because it is more cost effective to reuse datasets rather than collect new samples.
