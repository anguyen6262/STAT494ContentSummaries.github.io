New approaches to population stratification in genome-wide association studies

Purpose

To review the advantages and disadvantages of methods for correcting stratification and determine the optimal method under certain conditions.

Methods

  • Structured Association: Can accurately correct stratification in datasets whos population structure is the only type of sample structure. The data is grouped in different subpopulations. Then the association statistics are computed. These steps are taken in software. Due to the large size of genome wide datasets these studies have high computational costs. However there is always new development of software to reduce these costs which would make structured association an excellent option for inferring population structure and computing association statistics.fail to account for family structure or cryptic relatedness
  • PCA primarily shows the effects of differential bias. It removes the confounding effect of when genotype isn’t associated with phenotype, but genotype and phenotype are both associated with ancestry. PCA creates a greater correction to data that have greater differences in allele frequencies. This makes sense because PC1 is created based on the greatest variance of the data points. Limitations include not modeling cryptic relatedness and family structure. This can be improved by dividing by lambdaGC, but the test statistics that account specifically for family structure will still not be accurate. This also wouldn’t work if family structure is in the sample could potentially produce spurious principal components. This is due to the lack of variance in genetic ancestry. A solution to this problem is to use SNP loadings from a different sample in the same study that does have family structure. This is only possible if the size of the dataset is large enough. fail to account for family structure or cryptic relatedness. Another limitation is that it only accounts for fixed effects and doesn’t take into account the relatedness of individuals. Variance can be because people are from different population structures(different genotypes, same family). People are still developing PCA to work for more studies. Currently the main solutions is deleting or not including some data.
  • Family-Based Association tests: They are immune to stratification because the alleles have come from the same genetic ancestry. However the limitation is that information between family is required to calculate the test statistic. One way to fix this limitation is to transform in between family information to a rank statistic then combining the results within family information. This ensures that the tests are completely immune to stratification.
